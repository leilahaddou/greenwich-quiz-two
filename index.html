Charles Burney founded a school on what we currently know as Burney Street.

Croom is an old English word for crooked.

4) For which Queen is the Queen's House named?

Photo by [Duncan]/Flickr Creative Commons

The house was commissioned by and built for Queen Anne.

5) A road used to run along this stretch of the cutty sark gardens. What was it called?

Photo from Google street view

Running up from Billingsgate Dock on the riverfront, the street was known as Billingsgate Street. It is now part of Cutty Sark Gardens.

6) The stretch of road between Greenwich Station and St Alfege Church used to be known as what?

Map of London depicting Greenwich in the late 1880s.

This stretch of Greenwich High Road was formerly known as London Street

7) Which of these is NOT a former name for our current Greenwich Theatre?

Photo by Jessica Mulley/Flickr Creative Commons

Greenwich Theatre has had several names, but not the Playhouse Theatre. The Greenwich Playhouse Theatre group were sadly evicted from their home by Belushi's ahead of the 2012 Olympic Games.

8) A new spine road on the former industrial estate by Greenwich DLR has been named Lovibond Lane. What did the Lovibond family run in Greenwich?

Photo via Cathedral Group Ltd.

Lovibond Lane is named after the Lovibond family who ran a brewery on the site of Davy's and had several out buildings on the site as well.

9) Nevada Street was previously known as what?

Nevada Street used to be known as Silver Street.

10) What was Bardsley Lane known as until 1909?

Image via Google street view

Bardsley Street was known as Lamb Lane.

11) Burgos Grove was previously named after which naval hero?

Image via Google street view

Burgos Grove was once named Wellington Grove.

12) Royal Hill has not always had such a regal title. What was formerly known as?

Photo by Andrew Bowden/Flickr Creative Commons

Renamed after Robert Royal who built a theatre in Greenwich in 1748, it was formerly known as Gang Lane.

13) Which town centre road was formerly known as Clarence Street?

College Approach was called Clarence Street and we used to have Clarence Hall along there.
